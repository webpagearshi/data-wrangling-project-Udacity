Analyse the WeRateDogs twitter data¶

We have gathered, assessed and cleaned the data for WeRateDogs twitter account. The timeline for data gathered is for before August 1st, 2017. WeRateDogs are 'Your only source for professional dog ratings', their words not mine. :) They have a fun rating system where the numerator is usually more than the denominator and the denominator is mostly 10. Using their twitter data we will be analysing whether some dog stages are more popular than others or whether some dog breeds are getting higher ratings, do dogs with higher ratings also get more retweets and likes. We will also find the tweets with the highest ratings, retweets or likes.

Above is a doggo realizing that you can stand in a pool. With a rating of 1.3 (13/10) this doggo is really popular with maximum retweets and likes.

In our analysis we can see that the value count for pupper is highest but the average retweet_count is for doggo. From our violinplot visualization we can see that the pupper has highest density around 0 and hence has the lowest average retweet count. Doggo stage has the maximum outliers, this means that some of the tweets have very high value of retweets. Hence we can say that doggo is the more popular dog stage as the shape of the graph shows that the frequency for retweet_count is spread over a large range of values.

In the scatter plot above we can see the positive correlation between retweet count and favorite count. We also observe that higher retweet counts and favorite counts have higher ratings as the dots are mostly pink with specs of blue and red in areas under higher retweet count and favorite count. Thus we can say that a tweet with higher rating is more likely to get more number of likes and retweets.
